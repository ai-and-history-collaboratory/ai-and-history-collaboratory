# Swedish Authority History: Thematic Research Guide

**Version:** 1.2
**Date:** 17 February 2026
**Purpose:** Companion reference for the `ra-mcp-collaborative-research` skill file. Provides a thematic index derived from the ra-mcp server's `get_table_of_contents` resource — a comprehensive guide to Swedish civil and military local and regional archive-creating authorities (*Historik för civila lokala och regionala myndigheter*, ed. Ralph Haglund, 2005).

Read this file when translating a user's research question into effective Swedish search terms for the `search_transcribed` and `search_metadata` tools.

**Related reference:** For legal and judicial terminology with historical context and source attribution, see `swedish-early-modern-legal-terminology.md`. The two files are complementary: this guide is organised by administrative domain and optimised for search strategy; the terminology file provides contextualised definitions drawn from academic literature.

---

## How to Use This Index

When a user asks about a historical topic, identify the relevant administrative domain below. The Swedish terms listed are effective search keywords for both `search_transcribed` and `search_metadata`. The archival context helps interpret results.

**Important:** The terms below are **modern Swedish standardised forms**. In archival transcriptions from the 17th–18th centuries, orthography will differ substantially (e.g. `häradsrätt` may appear as `häradz rätt` or `herredz rätt`; `hovrätt` as `hofrätt` or `HofRätten`). When searching `search_transcribed`, try both modern and archaic forms. When searching `search_metadata`, modern forms typically work as catalogue descriptions use standardised spelling.

| Prosecutors | `åklagare` | Public prosecution |
| Rural courts | `häradsrätt`, `tingsrätt`, `ting` | Local courts handling crimes and disputes in the countryside |
| Special courts | `kommissorialrätt`, `hallrätt`, `borgrätt` | Ad hoc commissions (e.g. witchcraft trials), trade courts |
| Urban courts | `rådhusrätt`, `kämnärsrätt`, `magistrat` | City courts and municipal justice |
